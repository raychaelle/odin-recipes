Grandma's Dixie Dinner Rolls

Ever had dinner rolls that were the "cherry on top" to any meal they were added to? Well, now you can. Grandm's Dixie Dinner Rolls compliment every dish, and could easily be a Sunday Dinner staple. Of course they're best served warm, but they're still just as yummy the next day. The dough can be made up to 3 days ahead of time, or you can wait until 4 hours before serving.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 pkg yeast
  • 1 cup softened butter
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 cups flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  1. Combine 1/4 cup warm water and 1 tsp sugar.
  2. Add the yeast and let disolve for 4-5 minutes.
  3. After bubbles form, combine softened butter and 3/4 cup warm water.
  4. Add the 2 beaten eggs, 1/2 cup sugar, flour, and salt.
  5. Mix well to make a soft dough. Add more flour if necessary.
  6. Grease a bowl and add the dough. Grease the top and cover with wax paper.
  7. Refrigerate the dough until 3-4 hours before serving.
  8. When ready to bake, roll out 1/4 of the dough at a time into a circle. (Doesn't have to be perfectly round.)
  9. Cut into 8 pie shaped wedges, and roll up tightly, beginning at the wide end.
  10. Let rise for 3 hours.
  11. Bake at 375 degrees for 10 minutes or until done.

Makes ~32 rolls.
